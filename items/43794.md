## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` has SecretRef/audit tooling as a mitigation path, but it still allows and persists plaintext credentials at rest in `openclaw.json` and `auth-profiles.json`; the repo threat model still lists token theft from config files as a high-risk residual issue and explicitly recommends encryption at rest.

## Evidence

The short-term roadmap repeats `R-005 Add token encryption at rest`.

## Risks / Open Questions

- The issue body is narrower than current reality: secrets are not only in `~/.openclaw/openclaw.json`, but also in per-agent `auth-profiles.json` and channel/provider state under `credentials/`.
- SecretRefs reduce exposure for some surfaces, but they are an alternative storage mode, not a general implementation of built-in credential encryption at rest.
